Why me

The same read I’d want on my own store.

Sixteen years in QSR operations — Shift Leader to Assistant Manager to General Manager — and I still run a store every week. I’ve built schedules, chased variances, survived health inspections, and closed more Friday rushes than I can count. This isn’t theory from a consultant’s deck.

Do I need to change my POS?
No. Nothing gets replaced, integrated, or repaired. Everything works alongside what you already run.
What if I already have reports?
You almost certainly do — that’s not the gap. Reports tell you what already happened. The walk-through finds what’s slipping while the week is still recoverable: the labor drift on Wednesday instead of after payroll, the vendor short at the door instead of at the monthly count.
What if I’m not ready for AI?
Then start here and stop here. The walk-through stands completely on its own — observed pain points, dollar estimates, fixes you can run with a clipboard. Plenty of the fixes in a typical assessment need no technology at all.
Is this for independents or QSRs?
Both. The method came from QSR — labor pacing, food cost, rush execution — but the leaks are the same in any shift-based restaurant. If your store runs on shifts, handoffs, and a P&L, it applies.
