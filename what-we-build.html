What We Build

McPherson AI helps small businesses map real workflows, identify the source of truth, and build narrow automated processes with clear human approval rules before anything goes live.

The work starts with the business process: where work slows down, what system owns the information, what automation should help with, what requires human approval, what stays blocked, and what proof should exist after the workflow runs.

The process

The actual product starts with a disciplined Workflow Map.

1

Consult

Start with one real workflow that is taking too much time, getting missed, or depending on memory.

2

Map

Current steps, people, source of truth, repeated manual work, failure points, approval points, sensitive-data boundaries, accountability gaps, and what should never be automated.

3

Check the source of truth

CRM, spreadsheet, calendar, inbox, task board, POS export, or vendor platform. Confirm API, export, and integration access before making build promises.

4

Define the accountability scope

What the automation can do, cannot do, what needs human approval, what data can be shown, what stays in the source system, what is logged, and what is blocked by default.

5

Coordinate vendor and API access

Outreach, authorization, doc review, token/access planning, read-only-first scope, and compliance questions for sensitive data.

6

Build the first workflow

Narrow first: read-only lookup, lead follow-up, appointment prep, weekly report, manager handoff, or approval-gated action.

7

Test and prove

Fake-data testing, blocked-action testing, approval testing, logging review, proof summary, what-ran notes, and a go-live checklist.

8

Expand only after it works

The delivery menu leads with proven patterns: CRM lookup and QSR operator systems. Anything novel is scoped through the Workflow Map before a fixed build commitment.

Observa inside the method

Accountability is part of the build.

Every serious automated workflow should answer: what ran, what evidence exists, what was approved, what was blocked, what failed, what changed, what needs review, and what should not be assumed.

Narrow scope

Each workflow gets a defined lane before the automation is designed.

Approval rules

Consequential writes, customer promises, sensitive data, or money-touching actions require human approval or stay blocked.

Readable proof

The output includes a proof summary a business owner can actually review.
