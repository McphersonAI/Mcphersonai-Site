Accountability stance

If automated work helps run part of a business, the business should be able to prove what happened.

The offer is not "add a chatbot." It is a controlled workflow build: one process, one source of truth, narrow permissions, human approval where it matters, blocked actions by default, and readable accountability after the workflow runs.

  • Start with one real workflow that is slow, missed, or memory-dependent.
  • Confirm which system owns the information before designing automated behavior.
  • Use read-only-first access and human approval before consequential action.
  • Report what ran, what evidence exists, what needs review, and what stayed blocked.
