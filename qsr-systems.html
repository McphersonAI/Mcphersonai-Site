Origin proof lane

QSR Systems

McPherson AI began in restaurant operations. QSR is still the origin proof lane: food cost, labor leaks, shift execution, audit readiness, weekly operating reviews, and manager handoffs.

Built from the floor

Sixteen years of operating pain, turned into controlled workflows.

Food cost and waste

Ordering, portioning, receiving, waste, and weekly variance patterns that usually show up after the week is already spent.

Labor leaks

Mid-week labor drift, scheduling assumptions, rush coverage, and the gap between planned hours and actual volume.

Shift execution

Opening, mid-shift, close, unresolved issues, and handoffs that stop dying between shifts.

What stays from the original offer

The QSR map is still practical, not theatrical.

  • Watch a real operating window and map how work actually moves.
  • Identify repeated failure points, approval gaps, and what should stay human-owned.
  • Separate no-cost process fixes from workflows worth monitoring continuously.
  • Use the QSR proof lane as a grounded pattern for other small-business workflows.
